Abstract

This paper explores the emerging field of face-based voice conversion, leveraging the unique relationship between an individual's facial features and their vocal characteristics.
We present a novel face-based voice conversion framework that particularly utilize the average fundamental frequency of target speaker derived solely from facial images.
Results demonstrate that our framework can generate speech of superior quality and can successfully leverage facial images to approximate the vocal characteristics of the target speaker.
Extensive analysis further confirms its ability of aligning facial features to voice attributes, including tracking of fundamental frequency.
